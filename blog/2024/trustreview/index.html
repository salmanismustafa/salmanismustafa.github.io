Book Review - Trust by Hernan Diaz

Created in March 29, 2024

2024   ·   review,   literature,   personal     ·   posts  

5/5

A marvel of a novel. Unlike anything I’ve really read before. Layered with depth, dimension, emotion and an astute observation of capital and its imposing influence on narratives.

The prose is elegant, yet the narrative has an addictively aggressive pace. It’s hard to find an author that can tow that remarkable line between literature and entertainment.

As you go deeper into the meta fiction at play here, layers of meaning and truth are slowly uncovered and the reveals are utterly satisfying.

Even the title is a clever play on the word Trust: in finance, in relationships, in narratives. And these aspects have been handled deftly and with nuance.

I loved this read.

Some highlights:

“He became fascinated with the contortions of money-how it could be made to bend back upon itself to be force fed its own body”

“she thought there was no greater violence than the one done to meaning”

“she was quick to understand that he was very much impressed with Helen’s lineage and that he hoped to attach an old name to his new money.”

“They doubt their right to hold someone else accountable for their happiness.”

“And what is choice but a branch of the future grafting itself onto the stem of the present?”

“Where perseverance and ingenuity once dwelled, apathy and despair now loiter.”

“Every life is organized around a small number of events that either propel us or bring us to a grinding halt. We spend the years between these episodes benefiting or suffering from their consequences until the arrival of the next forceful moment.”

“I have no country. I don’t want one. The root of all evil, the cause of every war - god and country.”

“Authority and money surround themselves with silence, and one can measure the reach of someone’s influence by the thickness of the hush enveloping them.”

“My job is about being right. Always. If I’m ever wrong, I must make use of all my means and resources to bend and align reality according to my mistake so that it ceases to be a mistake.”

“That’s what they think friendship means: the freedom to turn you into a topic of conversation.”

“For I’ve come to think one is truly married only when one is more committed to one’s vows than the person they refer to.”

“Something miraculous and sad about the glass on the table. Water disciplined into a vertical cylinder. The depressing spectacle of our triumph over the elements.”

“God is the most uninteresting answer to the most interesting questions.”
